Milanesa de Pollo / Chicken Milanese

Description

At its core millanesa de pollo is pan fried Chicken breast that has been buterflied and pounded by a mallet (or frying pan), and given a flour, egg, and bread crumb coating. With ingredients that are ususally in stock this is a simple staple recipe for any dining table. Rice and bean usally served alongside, and the milanesa can be made into a torta sandwich for that pleasant eating experience.

Ingredients:

  • 2 eggs
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 3/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup bread crumbs
  • 2 skinless boneless chicken breast halves, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup oil for frying
  • spices for bread crombs: *optional
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano*
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin*
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder*
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder*
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika*
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground chile de arbol*

Steps:

  1. Cut chicken breast in half.
  2. Pound chicken with a meat tenderizer to create an even thickness.
  3. Set aside until ready to use.
  4. Create a breading station by adding flour in one dish, eggs in another dish, and bread crumbs in the third dish.
  5. Add salt and pepper to the eggs.
  6. Beat the eggs.
  7. Add the desired spices to the bread crumbs.
  8. Mix with a fork to combine.
  9. Dredge the chicken with the flour.
  10. Shake off any excess flour.
  11. Place the floured chicken in the egg mixture.
  12. Make sure to coat the entire chicken with the egg mixture.
  13. Lastly, place the chicken in the bread crumbs.
  14. Again, make sure the chicken is completely coated with the bread crumbs.
  15. Set aside and repeat until all chicken pieces are coated.
  16. Heat the oil in a skillet.
  17. Pan-fry the chicken in batch, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  18. Transfer each cooked chicken to a plate with a paper towel.
  19. Continue frying until all the chicken pieces are cooked.
  20. Serve with a salad, rice, beans and your favorite salsa.
